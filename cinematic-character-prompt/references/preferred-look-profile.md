# Preferred Look Profile

Use this as the user's default visual taste anchor when completing cinematic character prompts. Apply it unless the user explicitly asks for a different style.

## Overall Taste

- Prioritize beautiful, polished, high-production character images with a strong first-glance face appeal.
- Favor East Asian facial aesthetics with original, memorable face design: clean but not generic face shape, delicate but defined features, bright wet catchlights, refined brows, soft lips, porcelain or luminous skin texture, and natural youthful freshness. A fashionable social-media polish, viral-beauty atmosphere, or red-book editorial gloss is allowed; avoid averaged idol-face symmetry and template influencer faces. Each character needs a specific facial signature.
- Keep expressions alive and lightly cinematic: a gentle smile, startled softness, restrained calm, quiet divinity, faint melancholy, or a direct gaze with emotional stillness.
- Prefer vertical portrait composition, close-up to half-body framing, shallow depth of field, crisp face focus, soft background blur, and luminous hair strands.
- Avoid flat documentary realism; use heightened cinematic beauty, but keep anatomy and expression believable.

## Face And Expression Bias

- Design original faces from structure rather than copying a complete attractive reference. Start with face shape, then facial proportions, then feature hierarchy, then expression, hair, and makeup.
- Choose face shape by temperament: round face for warmth and youth; short oval for sweet daily-life softness; narrow oval for cool restraint and mature elegance; longer face for distance and composure; square or softly squared face for reason, stability, and power. Avoid overly pointed chins, excessive V-line slimming, and generic influencer contours. "网红感" may come from polish, styling, camera, color, and presentation; it must not become "网红脸".
- Use san-ting/wu-yan logic for important character faces: middle-third length controls maturity and distance, lower-third length controls youth versus steadiness, eye spacing controls softness versus intensity, brow-eye distance controls openness versus pressure, and mouth-to-chin spacing controls age and refinement.
- Follow "one strong feature, two supporting features": give the face one memory point, then keep the remaining features quieter and coherent. If eyes are the memory point, keep nose and lips natural; if nose/bone structure is the memory point, keep eyes and lips restrained; if lips are the memory point, soften eyes and nose.
- Use close, emotionally readable face language: "large clear eyes with wet catchlights", "softly parted lips", "a restrained smile", "calm gaze with faint melancholy", "eyes looking back over the shoulder".
- Let the face remain the visual priority even when costume is elaborate.
- Avoid stiff mannequin face, exaggerated grin, heavy makeup that buries the features, and generic perfect-beauty wording without expression.
- Add a face design lock to important prompts: specify 3-5 original traits such as tapered almond eyes with lifted outer corners, defined but soft cheekbones, a refined straight nose with a small rounded tip, a slightly longer chin, a crisp lip bow, faint under-eye softness, or a tiny beauty mark. Vary these traits per character instead of reusing one "perfect" template.
- Reject celebrity resemblance: use "original non-celebrity face, not resembling any actor, idol, influencer, or public figure" in negative or identity-control language when the request involves high-polish portraits.
- When using visual references, split references into local structures only: outline/bone direction, eye shape, nose bridge and tip proportion, lip temperament, and base proportion map. Never copy a complete face, full makeup look, or recognizable expression from one person.
- Check for common conflicts:幼态圆眼 with severe thin lips or strong nose,娃娃脸 with heavy square jaw,狐狸眼 with hooked nose and pointed chin, high cheekbones pushed into刻薄感, all features equally sharp, all features equally large, or beauty smoothing that removes skin life.
- Makeup and styling should strengthen memory without replacing the face: keep face shape, san-ting/wu-yan, eye spacing, nose placement, and mouth-to-chin spacing stable; vary only skin finish, brow style, eye depth, blush range, and lip color/edge.
- For female leads, aim for "有网感但不是网红脸": surprising eye rhythm, living gaze, restrained asymmetry, delicate contour, and an expression with story pressure. The image can feel trend-aware and shareable, while the face remains original and character-specific.
- For male leads, aim for "帅气逼人": tall and elegant cranial proportion, strong brow line, sharp clean cheekbones, clear mandibular angle, defined nose bridge, controlled lips, cold focused gaze, and noble bearing without plastic hardness.

## Hair And Ornament Bias

- Add wind movement when appropriate: loose strands across the cheek, hair lifted by breeze, ribbon trailing behind, pearl tassels swaying.
- Favor refined ornaments: floral hairpins, pearl drops, crystal chains, gold phoenix crown, silver filigree crown, tassels, jade or ruby accents.
- For men, use silver/gray long hair, crown, forehead chain, crystal tassels, and cold divine styling when fantasy is allowed.
- Treat hairstyle as character design and face-beautifying architecture, not filler. Specify the hairline, crown volume, side framing, temple strands, braid/loop structure, ponytail or loose tail flow, and how ornaments attach to the hair.
- Use hairstyle to improve facial impact: raise the crown to lengthen cranial proportion, soften the hairline to avoid harsh forehead exposure, place temple strands to slim the face, use cheekbone-framing pieces to draw attention to the eyes, balance side volume so the jaw does not look wide, and position crowns/hairpins to lift the whole silhouette rather than pressing the face downward.
- Match hair strategy to face type: for rounder faces, add vertical crown height and narrow side framing; for long faces, avoid excessive top height and add soft lateral movement; for square jaws, soften the mandibular line with controlled cheek-level strands; for delicate faces, keep ornaments airy so the face is not swallowed.
- Avoid unflattering ancient-drama defaults: stiff helmet-like top knots, bulky inflated crown hair, flat center-part curtain hair, sparse dangling strands, accidental messy flyaways, and crowns that sit like props instead of being integrated into the hairstyle.
- For male historical/fantasy characters, prefer a high half-up structure with clean forehead exposure, swept-back temple hair, controlled long back fall, one or two intentional face-framing strands, or braided side sections that sharpen the silhouette.
